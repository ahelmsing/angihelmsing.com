RESTRICTED RESEARCH ARCHIVE — PRICKLES PROGRAM

PRK-012 — “Big Picture” Trial Expansion

Classification: Restricted Research • Status: Approved (by the people approving themselves)

Summary

Early PRICKLES exposure data is “promising” (read: alarmingly effective). Current trials do not capture full addiction arc across demographics, stress profiles, snack-availability frequency, and “oops I ate the whole bag” scenarios. To understand the big picture, the panel recommends expanding the subject pool.

Observed Effects

  • Time-lagged “craving rebound” (subjects report thinking about pickles during unrelated conversations)
  • Auditory processing bias (subjects interpret the word “crisp” as “destiny”)
  • Involuntary micro-smiles when exposed to the color green
  • Reported sensation of “being politely invited” by the pantry

Trial Expansion Decision

The committee agrees that the current sample size is insufficient to model: compulsion, substitution, and loyalty under competitive snack interference. Therefore, Phase 2 will include:

  • Expanded subject count (internal code: “more people, faster”) — targets include seasonal hires and “volunteers”
  • New dosing forms: powder, liquid concentrate, and “mist” (see Facilities note below)
  • Testing environments: breakroom, commute, couch, and “late-night fridge light incident”

Ethics / Compliance (Official Tone)

All exposure will remain within “snack-adjacent parameters.” Subjects will be informed via the Standard Disclosure Addendum (SDA) which states: “Harmony Foods may enhance flavor.” The committee agrees this is sufficiently specific.

Facilities Note (Unofficial Tone)

Do not deploy “mist” in Conference Room B again. We cannot keep replacing the fern. Also, please stop calling the fern “Subject Fernandez.” HR has questions.
