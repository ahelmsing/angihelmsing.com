PRK-010 — Revenue Spike Analysis

Classification: Internal / Strategy Review
Status: Ongoing

Overview

Following the FP-77 release window (Ref: PRK-009), Product Strategy initiated a limited review of consumer behavior associated with micro-variance exposure within the Pebble Pops fruit line.

Key Observations

  • Repeat purchase rate increased by 218% in pilot regions
  • Average unit per transaction rose from 1.6 to 3.9
  • Consumer dwell time at shelf locations increased by 41%
  • Brand substitution behavior observed across adjacent snack categories

Preference Anchoring

Data indicates formation of rapid brand anchoring following minimal exposure. Once established, preference persists even when product is temporarily unavailable, redirecting purchase behavior rather than deferring it.

Scalability Assessment

Modeling suggests that controlled deployment at sub-threshold concentrations could influence market share across multiple Harmony brands without triggering conventional regulatory review thresholds.

Recommendations

  • Continue variance-controlled testing under “flavor enhancement” classification
  • Expand monitoring to GoldenVale and select Nova Harvest derivative lines
  • Limit documentation visibility to Strategy, Product Integrity, and Executive

Risk Notes

While current deployment remains within compliance margins, sustained preference shaping may invite long-term scrutiny if pattern recognition occurs outside controlled datasets.
