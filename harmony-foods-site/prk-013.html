RESTRICTED RESEARCH ARCHIVE — PRICKLES PROGRAM

PRK-013 — Consolidated Findings & Commercial Trigger Plan

Classification: Restricted Research • Status: “Let’s Not Call It What It Is”

What We Know (So Far)

  • PRICKLES flavor profile + additive creates reliable craving response after 1–3 exposures
  • Subjects display “snack loyalty,” even when presented with superior options (jury still out)
  • Micro-dose exposure increases repeat purchase probability beyond statistical sanity
  • Word association drift: “pickle” → “comfort” → “home” → “I should buy more”

Working Theory

The additive does not “force” behavior. It suggests it — the way a friend suggests you “just try one” and then you wake up hugging an empty bag like it’s a rescue puppy.

Commercial Trigger Plan (Draft)

The media team proposes a short-form spot designed to pair the additive’s craving curve with a “safe” visual/audio stimulus package. In non-technical terms:

  • A repeating jingle with a rhythm matching the craving rebound window
  • Color palette tuned to “green recognition happiness” (don’t ask why it works… it just does)
  • Three key phrases tested for maximum compliance without sounding evil:
    • “You deserve the crunch.”
    • “Just one more.”
    • “Bring the jar closer.” (Rejected. Too honest.)
  • Optional voiceover: warm, friendly, “your favorite aunt who definitely has secrets”

Internal Suggestions

“If we put the jingle behind a slow-motion pickle crisp falling into ranch, people will lose their minds.”
— Marketing, 09:12

“Please stop saying ‘activate the snack response.’ It makes everyone feel weird.”
— Legal, 09:14

“Counterpoint: feeling weird is our new KPI.”
— Someone whose email signature is just the word ‘SYNERGY’, 09:16

Next Step

Build distribution scale and finalize the broadcast path. Once the commercial runs at full strength, demand becomes self-propagating. The only risk is overshooting the curve and creating a “snack stampede event.” (See PRK-014.)
